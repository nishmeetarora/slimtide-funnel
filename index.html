Three gut-derived peptide hormones — GLP-1, GLP-2, and GLP-3 — govern your appetite, metabolism, and fat storage. Most diets ignore all three. Slimtide doesn't.

GLP-1 · Appetite Control

The Hunger Switch

GLP-1 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-1) is released by your gut after eating. It signals your brain to stop hunger, slows digestion, and keeps blood sugar stable — preventing the insulin spikes that convert food directly into fat.

What goes wrong:

In people with insulin resistance and PCOS, GLP-1 response is blunted — your brain never receives the "full" signal. You eat more, store more, and blame willpower.

Suppresses appetite naturally

GLP-2 · Gut Restoration

The Gut Healer

GLP-2 (Glucagon-Like Peptide-2) repairs and maintains your intestinal lining. A damaged gut wall causes chronic inflammation — which directly triggers cortisol release and drives stubborn belly fat that no calorie deficit can touch.

What goes wrong:

Years of crash diets, stress, and processed food erode your gut lining. Toxins leak into the bloodstream, triggering a low-grade inflammatory state that makes fat loss nearly impossible.

Heals leaky gut & reduces inflammation
Optimises nutrient absorption
Lowers cortisol-driven fat storage
GLP-3 · Metabolic Engine

The Fat Burner

GLP-3 (Oxyntomodulin) works alongside GLP-1 to elevate resting metabolic rate and directly increase energy expenditure — your body burns more calories even at rest, without gym sessions or extreme exercise.

What goes wrong:

Thyroid dysfunction and chronic stress suppress GLP-3 activity. Your metabolism slows year after year — each failed diet making the next one harder.
